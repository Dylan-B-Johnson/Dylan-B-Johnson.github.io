Interlaken

Interlaken is a small town located on a strip of land between the Brienzersee and Thunersee lakes (hence the name) in the Swiss Alps. Both lakes are formed from glacial melt. Erosion produces fine silt particles that remain in suspension in the waters, resulting in beautiful turquoise water. Interlaken is a huge tourist destination, and people come from all over Europe and the world to skii, hike, and paraglide.

A popular destination from Interlaken is Grindelwald and Lauterbrunnen, which sit at the base of the Eiger mountain. The Eiger's North face is the largest in the Alps, with 5,900ft of rock and ice. It was first climbed in 1938, and since than at least 64 climbers have died attempting it. This fact has won it the nickname Mordwand, literally "murder wall".

Things to do in Interlaken:

  • Go swimming! The Kifferinseli is a great public park where you can swim in the summer (fair warning, it will be cold).
  • Hike the Eiger trail! This trail goes along the bottom of the Eiger North face, and on a clear day, you may see climbers.
  • Wander the town! The best way to experience any town is to walk around, you never know what you will discover!
